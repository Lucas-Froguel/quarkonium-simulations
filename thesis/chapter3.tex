\chapter{Results}
\label{ch:results}

This chapter reports the numerical results obtained by applying the
four computational strategies of \cref{ch:framework} to the
spin-dependent charmonium Hamiltonians of \cref{ssec:matrices}: exact
diagonalisation (classical reference), VQE in the direct (2-qubit) and
Jordan--Wigner (4-qubit) encodings, VQITE, and TTITE. We first run a
noiseless sanity check on a simulator (\cref{sec:noiseless}), then
re-execute the same protocols under depolarising noise to characterise
the resilience of each method (\cref{sec:noisy}). A brief summary
(\cref{sec:results-summary}) closes the chapter.

% ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Experimental setup}
\label{sec:results-setup}

All quantum computations run through Qiskit's
\texttt{AerSimulator}. Pauli expectation values are
estimated with $N_{\mathrm{shots}} = 8192$ shots per term; VQITE
overlaps for the metric tensor use $4\!\times\! N_{\mathrm{shots}}$. The
classical optimiser for VQE is COBYLA with a maximum of $300$
iterations. VQITE uses $40$ forward-Euler imaginary-time steps with
$\Delta\tau = 0.05$ and Tikhonov regularisation $\lambda = 10^{-3}$.
TTITE uses total imaginary time $\tau_{\mathrm{total}} = 5$ with step
$\Delta\tau = 0.02$ and Taylor order $R = 10$. The depolarising channel
of \cref{eq:depolarizing} is applied to every gate when the noisy
backend is in use; rates swept are
$p \in \{0.0, 0.005, 0.01, 0.02, 0.05\}$ with three independent repeats
per configuration. A random seed (42) makes the runs reproducible. All
energies below are reported in inverse-femtometres; multiplication by
$\hbar c = 197.326$~MeV$\cdot$fm converts to MeV.

The full implementation, including the Hamiltonian builders, the three
algorithm wrappers, the noise sweep driver, and the standalone
visualisation script that produces every figure in this chapter, is
released as open-source software at
\url{https://github.com/Lucas-Froguel/quarkonium-simulations}~\cite{froguel_quarksim},
together with all configuration files needed to reproduce the runs
reported below.

Three patterns emerge. First, TTITE consistently produces a
high-fidelity ground state (\,$\mathcal{F} \gtrsim 0.9999$ on every
channel), reflecting the fact that the algorithm does not depend on
ansatz expressibility; its $\mathcal{O}(10^{-2})$~fm$^{-1}$ energy
error is dominated by the shot-noise estimator of the final
expectation value rather than by any algorithmic bias. Second, the
two VQE variants agree to within $\sim 10^{-2}$~fm$^{-1}$ across all
channels, confirming that the Jordan--Wigner encoding faithfully
reproduces the physics encoded by the direct mapping. Third, VQITE is
the most volatile of the three: on ${}^1S_0$ and ${}^3S_1$ it
recovers the true ground state with fidelity above $0.9999$, but on
${}^1P_1$ the local McLachlan descent on the three-parameter
manifold falls into an unphysical region (final energy $7.42$, well
above any low-lying eigenvalue, fidelity $\sim 0.17$). The failure is
a known sensitivity of VQITE to the initial parameter point and to
shot noise on the quantum metric estimate; VQE on the same ansatz is
not similarly trapped because COBYLA samples a trust region and can
escape such basins.

\subsection{Computational cost}
\label{ssec:noiseless-walltime}

\Cref{fig:wall_time} reports the wall-clock time per noiseless
ground-state computation, extracted from the run logs. Three orders of
magnitude separate TTITE, which on a noiseless statevector backend
needs only a single chain of LCU circuits and a final shot-based energy
measurement, from VQITE, whose imaginary-time loop estimates a fresh
metric tensor (six overlap circuits) and a fresh parameter-shift
gradient (six energy evaluations) at every step. VQE-direct and VQE-JW
sit between them, with VQE-JW $\sim 20\%$ slower because of the
deeper UCC circuit on twice as many qubits.

\subsection{Physical interpretation: meson masses}
\label{ssec:meson_masses}

The Hamiltonian of \cref{ssec:matrices} is the internal binding
Hamiltonian of the charm--anticharm pair, so the predicted meson mass
is obtained by adding back the rest energy of the two constituent
quarks,
\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:meson_mass}
  M_{\mathrm{meson}}
  \;=\; 2\,m_c + E_0 \cdot \hbar c,
\end{equation}
with $m_c = 1.4794$~GeV and $\hbar c = 197.326$~MeV$\cdot$fm. Applying
\cref{eq:meson_mass} to the exact-diagonalisation eigenvalues yields
the predictions of \cref{tab:meson_masses}, which we compare with the
experimentally measured masses of the corresponding mesons from the
Particle Data Group~\cite{pdg2024}.

\centering

  diagonalisation of \cref{eq:H_1S0,eq:H_3S1,eq:H_1P1} via
  \cref{eq:meson_mass}, compared with experimental values for the
  corresponding mesons~\cite{pdg2024}. All masses in MeV.}
\label{tab:meson_masses}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{lllrrr}
\toprule
Channel & Meson & $E_0$ (fm$^{-1}$) & $M_{\mathrm{pred}}$
        & $M_{\mathrm{exp}}$ & $\Delta M$ \\
\midrule
${}^1S_0$ & $\eta_c$ & $0.391$ & $3036$ & $2984$ & $+52$ \\
${}^3S_1$ & $J/\psi$ & $0.752$ & $3107$ & $3097$ & $+10$ \\
${}^1P_1$ & $h_c$    & $2.781$ & $3508$ & $3525$ & $-17$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The agreement with experiment is at the $10$--$50$~MeV level, typical
for a four-state harmonic-oscillator truncation of the Cornell model.
A particularly stringent test is the
$J/\psi$--$\eta_c$ hyperfine splitting, where the difference of the
two predictions cleanly cancels the rest-mass offset:
\begin{align*}
  \Delta M_{\mathrm{hf}}^{\mathrm{pred}}
    &= (E_0^{{}^3S_1} - E_0^{{}^1S_0})\,\hbar c
    = 71.2~\mathrm{MeV},\\
  \Delta M_{\mathrm{hf}}^{\mathrm{exp}}
    &= M_{J/\psi} - M_{\eta_c}
    = 113~\mathrm{MeV}.
\end{align*}
The model under-predicts the splitting by roughly $40$~MeV, a known
artefact of using a Gaussian-regularised contact term without the full
spin--orbit corrections of \cref{eq:hyperfine}.

spectrum are recovered cleanly whenever the underlying state is.

Two structural features of \cref{tab:noise-energies} deserve emphasis.
First, the TTITE column hits a hard ceiling at $\mathrm{Tr}(H)/4$
(values $4.27$, $4.44$, $5.80$ for the three channels) with zero
variance for every $p > 0$: the density matrix has fully decohered to
the maximally mixed state and no further information about the
spectrum survives. Second, the VQE-JW column rises about twice as fast
with $p$ as the VQE-direct column on every channel, a direct
fingerprint of its deeper UCC circuit. VQITE shows the largest
fluctuations across repeats ($\sigma \gtrsim 1$ at $p = 5\%$ on
${}^1S_0$ and ${}^3S_1$), consistent with noise corrupting both the
parameter-shift gradient and the finite-difference metric tensor at
every step.

The four methods degrade in qualitatively different ways. Among the
variational algorithms, the direct-encoding VQE is the most resilient:
its shallow $R_y$\,--\,CNOT ansatz (\cref{fig:woloshyn_ansatz}) is
exposed to only five gates per Pauli expectation, and the gradient-free
optimiser absorbs the residual shot+gate noise into the search. The
Jordan--Wigner VQE, despite using the same VQE skeleton, degrades much
faster: the UCC ansatz of \cref{fig:ucc_ansatz} chains an $X$ gate, six
CNOTs and three controlled-$R_y$ rotations on twice as many qubits, so
the noisy Pauli estimator at the optimum is biased by an amount that
grows roughly linearly with $p$. At $p = 0.05$ the energy error of
VQE-JW is $\sim\!3$~fm$^{-1}$ on every channel, whereas VQE-direct stays
below $\sim\!1.5$~fm$^{-1}$. The encoding choice that was harmless in
the noiseless regime thus becomes the dominant factor on noisy
hardware, and confirms a recurring lesson of NISQ-era variational
algorithms: circuit depth is the noise dial.

VQITE degrades faster still, because the metric tensor and
parameter-shift estimates of \cref{eq:metric_fd,eq:param_shift} both
require differences of noisy expectation values, so the effective
signal-to-noise ratio of every step drops more sharply with $p$ than for
a direct energy minimisation; the fidelity drops below $0.3$ by
$p = 0.05$.

TTITE collapses outright. With $\sim\!2{,}250$ sequential noisy gates
over a full Trotter trajectory and an ancilla-reset protocol that
\emph{cannot} reject failed LCU branches on real hardware, the work
register relaxes toward the maximally mixed state $\mathrm{Tr}(H)/4$,
and the fidelity drops to ${\sim}\,0.25$ (random guessing in the four
dimensional Hilbert space) at any non-zero noise rate. The two
imaginary-time methods that were superior in the noiseless regime are
therefore the most fragile under depolarising noise.

\section{Summary}
\label{sec:results-summary}

In the noiseless regime all four methods recover the channel
eigenvalues, but they are not interchangeable: TTITE is essentially
exact (fidelity above $0.9999$ everywhere, energy error limited only
by shot noise in the final Pauli measurement), VQE in either encoding
is limited by the chosen ansatz at the $\sim 10^{-2}$~fm$^{-1}$ level,
and VQITE recovers the ground state on ${}^1S_0$ and ${}^3S_1$ but
falls into a local-descent trap on ${}^1P_1$. The transition amplitudes
$|\langle\eta_c|J/\psi\rangle|^{2}$ and $|\langle\eta_c|r|h_c\rangle|$
are recovered to within $1\%$ of the classical reference for every
method whose ground state is correct, confirming that the radiative
observables of charmonium follow the same accuracy ranking as the
energies.

Under depolarising noise the ranking inverts and sharpens: the
shallow-circuit VQE-direct is the most resilient, the deeper-circuit
VQE-JW degrades to errors of $\mathcal{O}(\text{fm}^{-1})$ at moderate
$p$, VQITE degrades further still, and TTITE collapses to the
maximally mixed state at any non-zero noise rate. The encoding choice
that was second-order in the noiseless regime becomes the dominant
factor in the noisy one. These observations motivate the
noise-mitigation strategies developed in the next chapter.
